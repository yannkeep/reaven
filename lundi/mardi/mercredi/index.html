Compromised Asset Distribution

Understanding the nature of the exfiltrated data is critical for damage assessment. The primary target was highly sensitive infrastructure blueprints, followed closely by raw customer personally identifiable information (PII). This distribution indicates a highly coordinated espionage effort rather than a generic financial skim.

Infrastructure Blueprints (42%)

Proprietary server configurations, API keys, and internal network topologies. Represents the highest long-term security risk.

Customer PII (28%)

Unencrypted databases containing names, physical addresses, and partial social security numbers.

Financial Ledgers (18%)

Internal payroll and vendor payment histories. Lower direct risk but high potential for secondary phishing campaigns.

Chronology of Exfiltration

The attack was not a single event but a sustained extraction over a 30-day period. Note the massive spike in volume during the third week, which corresponds with the compromise of the secondary backup servers. The initial low-volume days represent the reconnaissance and lateral movement phase.

Exploited Vectors

A ranking of the system vulnerabilities utilized by the threat actors. Legacy authentication systems proved to be the weakest link, accounting for the vast majority of initial breaches, highlighting the urgent need for zero-trust architecture.
