Solution

The €24,000 Alternative

Transform €21 billion in forced inactivity into active citizen contribution. The technology exists. The legal framework exists. What's missing is political will.

Belgium spends €21-24 billion annually — approximately €1 million per hour — to maintain 978,000 people in forced inactivity. The system is designed to manage poverty, not solve it.

The Ouaisfieu project proposes an alternative: Provisioned Contributive Economy (ECP). Instead of paying people to stay inactive, enable them to contribute — without losing benefits.

The Problem: The Poverty Trap

Belgium's social system was designed for a binary labor market: you're either employed or unemployed. It cannot process intermediate situations.

If you're receiving benefits and earn €1, you can lose benefits worth more than €1. This creates marginal tax rates exceeding 100% — a mathematical trap that punishes any attempt to contribute.

The Arizona budget's response: cut benefits to "incentivize" work. But for 978,000 people, the barriers aren't motivational — they're structural: health, age, skills, mobility, discrimination.

The Solution: Provisioned Contributive Economy

How ECP Works

1

Contribution without employment

People contribute to civic projects (citizen monitoring, participatory science, community support) under the 2005 volunteering law framework.

2

Deferred provisioning

Instead of immediate payment (which triggers benefit loss), value is provisioned for future use — education, health, services.

3

Fiscal ruling validation

The SDA (Service des Décisions Anticipées) confirms the arrangement doesn't constitute taxable income or employment.

4

Benefits preserved

No ONEM requalification as employment (no subordination, no remuneration, no work contract).

Legal Architecture

Existing Legal Frameworks Used

  • 2005 Volunteering Law: €1,692.51 annual ceiling for volunteer expenses
  • ONSS criteria: No employment contract = no social security contribution
  • SDA fiscal ruling: Established procedure for tax certainty
  • Article 344 CIR 92: Anti-abuse provision compliance
  • VVPR-bis regime: For any future equity participation

This isn't legal fiction. Each component exists and is used daily in Belgium. The innovation is combining them to solve the poverty trap.

What Would ECP Enable?

→ Citizen monitoring: Democratic oversight, transparency projects
→ Participatory science: Environmental monitoring, data collection
→ Community support: Mutual aid, local services
→ Knowledge production: Documentation, translation, research assistance
→ Cultural contribution: Heritage preservation, artistic production

978,000 people represent millions of hours of potential contribution — currently wasted because the system can only process "employment" or "inactivity."

Why Isn't This Happening?

Ideological lock: The Arizona coalition's philosophy is that only market employment creates value. Contribution outside employment is invisible.

Political economy: Social security funding is indexed to traditional employment. Alternative contribution models threaten the financing model.

Path dependency: The system is optimized for its current dysfunction. Reform requires admitting the current approach fails.

The Alternative Exists

Whether this specific solution is implemented or not, the point stands: €21 billion in forced inactivity is a choice, not a necessity. Alternatives are technically and legally feasible.

The question isn't "can we?" — it's "will we?"
