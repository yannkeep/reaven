How Belgium's state became a serial offender against its own courts

Since 2021, Belgium has faced a structural asylum reception crisis. The response has been extraordinary: systematic non-compliance with judicial orders. The state pays fines rather than obey the law.

Why Does This Happen?

Belgium's particratic system shields the executive from accountability. The majority coalition controls parliament through party discipline. No vote of censure. No investigation commission. No ministerial resignation.

The state treats court fines as an operational cost — cheaper than building reception capacity or reforming the system. Justice becomes transactional: pay to violate.

This represents a fundamental breakdown of rule of law. When the state itself becomes the most prolific violator of court orders, the social contract fractures.
