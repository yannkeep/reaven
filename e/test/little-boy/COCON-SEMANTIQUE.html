Part I: A State Above Its Own Law

Since 2021, Belgium has faced a structural asylum reception crisis, leaving thousands of people homeless due to insufficient capacity in the Fedasil network. Courts have repeatedly ordered the government to provide shelter as required by law.

The government's response has been extraordinary: systematic non-compliance. Rather than expanding capacity or reforming the system, the Belgian state has chosen to pay fines while ignoring judicial rulings.

"When a state systematically ignores its own courts, it operates as a rogue state. The rule of law exists only on paper." ‚Äî Belgian magistrate, speaking on condition of anonymity, 2025

This pattern extends beyond asylum policy. In the landmark Klimaatzaak case (2021), Belgium was ordered to reduce CO2 emissions in line with international commitments. As of 2026, the state remains in non-compliance, with massive fines looming.

The Particracy Problem

Analysts point to Belgium's unique political system as a key factor. Known as "particracy," it concentrates power not in elected representatives, but in party structures ‚Äî specifically, party presidents who negotiate coalitions, appoint ministers, and impose voting discipline on parliamentarians.

"Parliamentary work has been reduced to pushing a button," admitted Belgian MP Michel De Maegd (MR) in 2025, describing how party discipline overrides individual judgment.

Part IV: Implications for the European Union

Belgium's crisis raises uncomfortable questions for the European project. As a founding member hosting EU institutions, Belgium has historically embodied European values of rule of law and social protection.

The situation creates a troubling precedent: if Belgium can systematically ignore court orders without EU intervention, what leverage exists for addressing similar issues in Hungary or Poland?

"The EU's credibility on rule of law depends on consistent application. Belgium cannot be exempt because it hosts the Commission." ‚Äî European democracy researcher, speaking to Ouaisfieu

The investigation found no evidence of formal EU proceedings against Belgium for rule of law violations, despite documented non-compliance with judicial rulings that would trigger Article 7 discussions if occurring in newer member states.
