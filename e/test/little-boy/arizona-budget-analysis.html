The Trajectory: 2025 → 2042

2025

Starting point: Deficit 5.4% GDP, debt 104% GDP. Arizona coalition takes office with €9.2B adjustment plan.

2026-2027

Phase 1: 90,000 unemployment benefit exclusions begin. CPAS (social services) face capacity crisis. Healthcare norm cut to 2% vs. 3.2% need.

2029

End of plan: Deficit still at ~5.4% despite cuts. Debt rising to 119% GDP. Interest payments approaching €19B/year.

2030

Projection: Deficit rises to 6.5% (€40B). Debt reaches 120% GDP. Interest payments at €21B/year — nearly double 2025 levels.

2035-2042

Convergence: Aging population peak + climate costs + debt spiral. Social safety net degraded precisely when needed most.

⚠️ The Snowball Effect

Interest payments will rise from €12.2B (2026) to €20.9B (2030) — a 70% increase. When interest rates exceed economic growth, debt becomes self-reinforcing. The National Bank warns this threshold is approaching.

Why Austerity Fails

The Arizona budget rests on three assumptions that evidence contradicts:

Assumption 1: "Cutting benefits creates jobs."
Reality: For 978,000 people in forced inactivity, barriers are health, age, skills, mobility, and discrimination — not motivation. Labor market data shows no correlation between benefit cuts and employment in this population.

Assumption 2: "€7.9B in return effects will materialize."
Reality: If destructive feedback loops activate (CPAS overload, health deterioration, consumption collapse), these projected returns vanish.

Assumption 3: "Social fabric will hold."
Reality: CPAS are already saturated. Social workers report burnout. Labor tribunals face backlogs. Each failure cascades.
